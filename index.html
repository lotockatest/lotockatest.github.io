Japanese flowers

Japan is home to some of the world’s most breathtaking flowers, each carrying a deep cultural significance. From delicate cherry blossoms that symbolize the fleeting nature of life to vibrant chrysanthemums that represent longevity and joy, Japanese flowers offer a rich tapestry of beauty and meaning. These blooms aren’t just admired for their aesthetic appeal; they hold a special place in art, literature, and everyday life.

Among them, the kiku (chrysanthemum) stands out as a symbol of longevity and joy. It is a perennial flowering plant with a wide variety of cultivars, chrysanthemums bloom in shades of yellow, white, red, and purple. Their petals can be single-layered or densely packed, creating a full, rounded appearance. Ajisai (hydrangea) is a deciduous shrub that produces large, globe-like clusters of flowers in shades of blue, purple, pink, or white. The color of the flowers changes depending on the acidity of the soil—acidic soil produces blue flowers, while alkaline soil results in pink blooms.
