Kiku (Chrysanthemum)

Scientific Name: Chrysanthemum morifolium
Blooming Season: Autumn (September–November)
Colors: Yellow, white, red, purple
While many think of cherry blossoms as Japan’s most iconic flower, the chrysanthemum holds a special place in Japanese history. It is the symbol of the Imperial Family, appearing on the official seal of the emperor. The flower represents nobility, rejuvenation, and long life, making it a central figure in Japanese culture.

Chrysanthemums also carry different meanings depending on their color:

Red chrysanthemums – "I love you" (a symbol of deep passion and affection).

White chrysanthemums – "Faithfulness" (often used in offerings and solemn occasions).

Yellow chrysanthemums – "A broken heart" (symbolizing grief or unrequited love).

These flowers are well-known for their longevity and resilience, making them popular in floral arrangements and gardens. Each autumn, Japan hosts Kiku Matsuri (Chrysanthemum Festivals), where intricate floral displays celebrate the beauty and prestige of this remarkable flower.
